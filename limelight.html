LIMELIGHT

FI-100 · v0.6.0

LIMELIGHT is a from-disk screen recorder. It composites a screen share, camera overlays, image overlays, and freehand annotation into a single video, mixes in the microphone and system audio you choose, and writes a .webm you download. Limelight was the intense stage light that lit a performer for the whole house, so the name fits a tool for putting your screen in front of the world.

Everything runs locally in the browser. No frames, audio, or recordings are uploaded anywhere. Files stay on this machine until you download and move them.

License: GPL-3.0

Third-party libraries: none. Hand-built HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Icons are inline SVG.

Feedback: report issues at greenshoegarage.com.

Streaming to YouTube and Twitch runs through SEMAPHORE, a small companion relay (semaphore.py) you run on the same machine. Those platforms ingest over RTMP, which a page at file:// cannot speak, so the relay does the RTMP push with ffmpeg. LIMELIGHT sends the composited feed to the relay over ws://localhost. Your stream keys live only in the relay's local config, never in this page, so nothing secret is stored in the file. Recording still works with no relay and no network at all.

Known limitations in v0.6.0: Chromium-based browsers recommended (screen and camera capture from file:// rely on the secure-context treatment Chromium gives local files). Output is WebM (Safari support is limited). Streaming needs SEMAPHORE plus ffmpeg installed; the relay transcodes to H.264/AAC, which adds a few seconds of latency. Single monitor/window per session. No PWA layer yet. Recordings are held in memory, so a very long take uses a lot of RAM.
